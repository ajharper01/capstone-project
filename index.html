Daydreams: Happen an average of 70-120 minutes a day. Classified as a level of consciousness between sleep and wakefulness.

Lucid Dreams: When you realize you are dreaming. Some can remain in the lucid state of dreaming and make decisions in their dreams influencing the dream’s outcome without awakening. Nightmares: Causes the dreamer to wake up feeling anxious and frightened maybe from a response to real life trauma and situations. An indication of a fear that needs to be acknowledged and confronted. It is our subconscious is saying, “Pay attention!” NOTE: This type of nightmare falls under a special category called Post-traumatic Stress Nightmare (PSN). Recurring Dreams: Recurring dreams repeat themselves because a conflict depicted in the dream remains unresolved or ignored. Healing Dreams: Healing dreams serve as messages for the dreamer regarding their health. Prophetic Dreams: Also known as precognitive or psychic dreams that seemingly foretell the future. Signal Dreams: Signal dreams help you how to solve problems or make decisions in your waking life. Epic Dreams: Epic dreams (or Great dreams) are so compelling, and so vivid that you cannot ignore them. These dreams possess much beauty and contain many archetypal symbology.
